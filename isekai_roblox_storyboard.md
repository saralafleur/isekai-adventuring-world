# Isekai Roblox Game Storyboard

## Working Title

**Echoes of Aetheria**

## Document Purpose

This document defines the opening story arc, cinematic structure, early quests, characters, environments, gameplay systems, dialogue beats, and implementation notes for a Roblox fantasy role-playing game inspired by traditional isekai anime.

It is written as a handoff document for an AI-assisted game development workflow. The material should be treated as a strong creative and structural foundation rather than a rigid final script. Character names, visual designs, quest values, combat balance, and technical details can be refined during production.

---

# 1. Core Game Concept

The player controls a teenage boy who was reincarnated into a medieval fantasy world filled with swords, magic, dragons, elves, dwarves, monsters, guilds, kingdoms, and ancient dungeons.

The protagonist was born into an ordinary peasant family and has lived in this world for approximately fourteen years. He has always experienced faint dreams and fragmented memories of another life, but he has never fully understood what they mean.

The story begins on the morning of his village's annual First Harvest Festival. The player completes several simple village tasks, meets the protagonist's family and closest friends, and learns the basic game mechanics.

After the introductory quests are completed, a group of bandits attacks the village and kidnaps the protagonist's female childhood friend. The protagonist and his male childhood friend leave the village to rescue her.

This event launches the main adventure.

---

# 2. Player Experience Goals

The opening should make the player feel:

1. **Curious** about the protagonist's previous life and mysterious memories.
2. **Emotionally connected** to the village, family, and childhood friends.
3. **Comfortable** with the game's movement, interaction, inventory, gathering, dialogue, and combat systems.
4. **Surprised** when the peaceful village sequence suddenly turns dangerous.
5. **Motivated** to rescue the kidnapped friend.
6. **Excited** to leave the village and explore the larger fantasy world.

The opening must not feel like a long, non-interactive movie. Cinematics should be brief, visually interesting, and broken up by playable sections.

---

# 3. Genre and Tone

## Genre

- Isekai anime
- Fantasy action RPG
- Story-driven adventure
- Open-zone exploration
- Quest-based progression
- Cooperative multiplayer potential

## Tone

The opening begins warm, peaceful, humorous, and nostalgic. It gradually introduces mystery, danger, and emotional urgency.

The game should include:

- Sincere friendships
- Light comedy
- Dramatic battles
- Ancient mysteries
- Heroic growth
- Occasional anime-style emotional moments
- A balance between serious story scenes and playful Roblox gameplay

The protagonist should not begin as an unstoppable hero. His power should develop gradually.

# 4. World Overview

## World Name

**Aetheria**

Aetheria is a large fantasy world divided into kingdoms, wild territories, magical forests, mountain strongholds, ruined civilizations, and unexplored lands.

Magic flows through the world in invisible currents called **Aether Lines**. Most people can use only a small amount of magic. Skilled mages learn to sense and manipulate these currents.

Ancient legends say that people from other worlds have appeared in Aetheria before. These individuals are known in forbidden records as **Worldwalkers**.

The existence of Worldwalkers is not common public knowledge.

## Starting Region

**The Kingdom of Valedorn**

Valedorn is a human kingdom known for farming, trade roads, knightly orders, and old ruins left behind by an ancient magical civilization.

## Starting Village

**Briar Glen**

Briar Glen is a small farming village surrounded by green hills, forests, streams, and wheat fields. It is remote enough to feel safe but close enough to a trade road that merchants and travelers occasionally pass through.

The village contains:

- The protagonist's family cottage
- A blacksmith
- A general store
- A small shrine
- A village square
- A watermill
- Farms and animal pens
- A training field
- A healer's cottage
- A watchtower
- A forest path
- A road leading toward the nearby town of Stonebridge

# 5. Main Characters

## 5.1 The Protagonist

### Working Name

**Kael Rowan**

The player's display name may replace Kael in dialogue. NPCs can refer to the player by a chosen name, while Kael remains the default story name.

### Age

14 years old

### Background

Kael was born into a peasant farming family in Briar Glen. He has always been intelligent, observant, and slightly unusual. He sometimes uses words or ideas that do not exist in Aetheria, although he does not understand why.

He experiences dreams of:

- Tall buildings made of glass
- Metal vehicles moving without horses
- Glowing screens
- Rain on city streets
- A hospital room
- A voice saying, “You still have something left to do.”

Kael does not initially remember his death or his identity in the previous world.

### Personality

- Curious
- Loyal
- Protective
- Sometimes sarcastic
- Brave when others are in danger
- Insecure about being ordinary
- Deeply afraid of losing the people he loves

### Starting Abilities

- Basic farming knowledge
- Basic sword handling from village training
- Minor gathering skills
- Weak magical sensitivity
- Unexplained ability to recognize ancient symbols

### Hidden Ability

**Echo Sight**

Echo Sight allows Kael to see fragments of memories embedded in objects, ruins, magical locations, or people connected to powerful emotions.

At the beginning of the game, Echo Sight activates unpredictably. Later, it becomes a core gameplay mechanic.

---

## 5.2 Male Childhood Friend

### Name

**Tarin Holt**

### Age

14 years old

### Background

Tarin is the son of Briar Glen's blacksmith. He has trained with wooden swords since childhood and dreams of becoming a knight.

### Personality

- Loud
- Confident
- Funny
- Competitive
- Fiercely loyal
- Acts before thinking
- Hides fear behind jokes

### Combat Role

- Melee fighter
- Uses a short sword and shield
- Can draw enemy attention
- Protects Kael during early combat

### Story Role

Tarin is the protagonist's closest male friend and first permanent party member. He joins Kael on the rescue mission immediately after the kidnapping.

### Character Conflict

Tarin wants to prove he is brave, but he secretly believes he is responsible for failing to protect the kidnapped girl.

---

## 5.3 Female Childhood Friend

### Name

**Lyra Vale**

### Age

14 years old

### Background

Lyra is the daughter of Briar Glen's healer. She is thoughtful, adventurous, and unusually talented with healing magic.

### Personality

- Kind
- Clever
- Calm under pressure
- Playfully teases Kael and Tarin
- More adventurous than her mother realizes
- Notices when Kael is hiding something

### Abilities

- Minor healing magic
- Herbal knowledge
- Basic light magic
- Strong magical potential

### Story Role

Lyra is kidnapped during the bandit attack. Her kidnapping appears random at first, but the bandits were specifically ordered to capture someone with her magical signature.

### Hidden Importance

Lyra possesses an inherited magical trait connected to an ancient seal beneath the kingdom.

The bandits do not understand the full reason she was selected.

---

## 5.4 Kael's Mother

### Name

**Mira Rowan**

A loving but practical farmer. She worries about Kael's strange dreams and unusual behavior but assumes he is simply imaginative.

She provides the player's first quest and introduces inventory interactions.

---

## 5.5 Kael's Father

### Name

**Daren Rowan**

A hardworking farmer and former militia scout. He rarely speaks about his past.

He teaches Kael basic sword controls during the tutorial. He is injured during the bandit attack but survives.

His old militia equipment later becomes important.

---

## 5.6 Village Elder

### Name

**Elder Bram Alder**

The village leader. He is kind, respected, and more knowledgeable about ancient legends than he admits.

He recognizes the symbol that briefly appears in Kael's eyes during the attack.

---

## 5.7 Bandit Leader

### Name

**Rook Veylan**

Rook leads the bandits who attack Briar Glen. He is not a mindless villain. He is a former soldier who now works for a hidden employer.

### Personality

- Controlled
- Tactical
- Ruthless when necessary
- Avoids unnecessary killing
- Distrusts magic
- Follows orders because someone he loves is being held hostage

### Visual Design

- Dark leather armor
- Red scarf
- Scar across one eyebrow
- Curved sword
- Metal gauntlet marked with a black sun emblem

### Story Function

Rook becomes a recurring antagonist and possible future ally.

The opening arc is divided into the following phases:

1. Reincarnation cinematic
2. Childhood montage
3. Player awakening and character introduction
4. Peaceful village tutorial quests
5. Friendship scenes
6. First hints of Echo Sight
7. Bandit attack
8. Kidnapping of Lyra
9. Aftermath and emotional decision

# 8. Detailed Storyboard

## Scene 1: The End of the Previous Life

### Scene Type

Opening cinematic

### Estimated Length

45 to 75 seconds

### Location

A modern city during heavy rain

### Story Purpose

- Establish that the protagonist lived in another world.
- Avoid revealing every detail of his previous identity.
- Introduce the emotional mystery behind his reincarnation.
- Create a dramatic anime-style opening.

### Visual Sequence

The screen begins black.

The sound of rain fades in.

A blurred modern city appears. Streetlights reflect across wet pavement. People move past under umbrellas. The camera is low and unstable, suggesting the protagonist is lying on the ground.

The player sees only fragments:

- A hand reaching toward a cracked phone
- Flashing emergency lights
- A silver pendant lying in the rain
- A shadow leaning over the protagonist
- A hospital ceiling
- A heart monitor
- A white light

The protagonist hears a distant voice.

### Voice

> “You were not supposed to end here.”

The screen flashes white.

### Additional Dialogue Fragments

> “Can you hear me?”

> “Stay with us!”

> “There is still something you must protect.”

The heart monitor becomes a long tone.

The sound transforms into the cry of a newborn baby.

### Audio Direction

- Heavy rain
- Distant traffic
- Muffled emergency voices
- Heart monitor
- Low orchestral tension
- Sudden silence before the baby cries

### Development Notes

The protagonist's face should not be shown clearly. This preserves mystery and makes it easier for players to project themselves into the role.

The cinematic should be skippable after the player's first viewing.

## Scene 2: Rebirth

### Scene Type

Cinematic montage

### Estimated Length

60 to 90 seconds

### Location

The Rowan family cottage and surrounding village

### Story Purpose

- Show that the protagonist was reborn as a baby.
- Establish the loving peasant family.
- Show his growth without requiring a long childhood chapter.
- Introduce Lyra and Tarin as lifelong friends.

### Visual Sequence

A newborn baby opens his eyes.

Mira holds him while Daren watches with relief.

The montage moves through several ages.

#### Age 2

Kael reaches toward a candle flame. The flame bends toward his hand before Mira quickly pulls him away.

#### Age 4

Kael draws a strange rectangular object on parchment. Daren asks what it is. Kael looks confused and says he does not know.

#### Age 6

Kael meets Tarin near the blacksmith. Tarin challenges him to lift a wooden practice sword and immediately drops his own sword on his foot.

#### Age 7

Lyra heals a cut on Kael's hand with weak golden magic. The three children become friends.

#### Age 9

The friends explore near the forest edge. Kael touches an old stone covered in runes. For one second, he sees a ruined city floating in the sky.

#### Age 12

Daren teaches Kael and Tarin to fight with wooden swords. Lyra watches and laughs when both boys fall into a haystack.

#### Age 14

Kael stands on a hill overlooking Briar Glen at sunrise.

A faint voice from his previous life whispers:

> “Wake up.”

### Transition

The screen fades into the player's bedroom.

## Scene 3: A Familiar Dream

### Scene Type

Playable introduction

### Location

Kael's bedroom in the Rowan cottage

### Time

Early morning

### Story Purpose

- Give the player control.
- Teach camera movement and basic interaction.
- Establish Kael's recurring dreams.
- Introduce the home environment.

### Opening

Kael wakes suddenly in bed.

For a moment, a translucent modern city skyline overlaps the wooden walls of his room. It disappears when he blinks.

### Internal Dialogue

> “That dream again.”

> “Rain. Lights. A voice.”

> “Why does it feel like a memory?”

### Player Objectives

1. Get out of bed.
2. Move around the room.
3. Inspect three optional objects.
4. Equip simple clothing.
5. Open the bedroom door.

### Interactable Objects

#### Crude Drawing

A childhood drawing of a glowing rectangular screen.

Kael says:

> “I drew this years ago. I still don't know what it is.”

#### Old Pendant

A pendant found with Kael when he was born. It resembles the pendant from the modern-world cinematic.

Kael says:

> “Mother says this was wrapped in my blanket when I was born.”

#### Wooden Practice Sword

A gift from Daren.

Kael says:

> “Tarin still insists he won our last duel.”

### Tutorial Systems

- Movement
- Camera control
- Object interaction
- Quest marker display
- Basic inventory
- Equipping an item

### Environmental Details

- Wooden walls
- Small bed
- Farming tools
- Family photos or painted portraits
- Morning sunlight through a small window
- Distant rooster sound
- Festival decorations visible outside

## Scene 4: Breakfast at the Rowan Home

### Scene Type

Dialogue and quest setup

### Characters

- Kael
- Mira
- Daren

### Story Purpose

- Establish family relationships.
- Introduce the festival.
- Give the player the first quest.
- Foreshadow Daren's combat experience.

### Cinematic Beat

Kael enters the main room. Mira is preparing bread and stew. Daren is repairing a leather bracer.

### Dialogue

**Mira:**

> “There you are. I was beginning to think you planned to sleep through the festival.”

**Kael:**

> “I had the dream again.”

**Mira:**

> “The city of glass?”

**Kael:**

> “And the rain.”

Daren pauses for a moment but does not look up.

**Daren:**

> “Dreams feel important until breakfast.”

**Mira:**

> “Your father believes food is the cure for everything.”

**Daren:**

> “It usually is.”

### Quest Assigned

**Quest: Harvest Morning**

Mira asks Kael to collect supplies needed for the festival.

### Quest Objectives

- Collect 5 sunroot vegetables from the family garden.
- Gather 3 eggs from the chicken pen.
- Deliver a basket to the village baker.

### Rewards

- 20 experience
- 15 copper coins
- Basic satchel
- Bread item

### Tutorial Systems

- Quest log
- Gathering
- Item pickup
- Objective tracking
- NPC dialogue
- Quest rewards

### Optional Dialogue

Before leaving, the player can ask Daren about the bracer.

**Daren:**

> “Old militia equipment. Nothing more.”

This hints that Daren has a more complicated past.

## Scene 5: Harvest Morning Quest

### Scene Type

Playable village tutorial

### Location

Rowan farm and nearby village streets

### Story Purpose

- Familiarize the player with Briar Glen.
- Introduce village NPCs.
- Create emotional investment in the peaceful setting.
- Teach gathering and delivery mechanics.

### Gameplay Sequence

#### Objective 1: Collect Sunroot

The player enters the garden and gathers five glowing orange vegetables.

Possible minor complications:

- One sunroot is stuck and requires repeated interaction.
- A farm dog steals one and runs a short distance.
- The player learns sprinting while chasing the dog.

#### Objective 2: Gather Eggs

The player enters the chicken pen.

A chicken named General Cluck blocks the final egg.

The player can:

- Distract it with grain.
- Carefully walk around it.
- Get pecked and trigger a humorous animation.

#### Objective 3: Deliver Basket

The player walks through the village toward the baker.

### Environmental Storytelling

Villagers prepare for the festival.

- Children hang ribbons.
- Merchants set up stalls.
- Musicians tune instruments.
- Guards decorate the watchtower.
- A traveling merchant's wagon enters the village.

### Background NPC Dialogue

> “The weather held. We may have a good harvest after all.”

> “Stonebridge traders should arrive before noon.”

> “Did you hear wolves near the eastern road?”

> “Probably nothing.”

This dialogue subtly foreshadows danger.

## Scene 6: Meeting Tarin and Lyra

### Scene Type

Character introduction and social scene

### Location

Village square

### Story Purpose

- Introduce both childhood friends during gameplay.
- Establish their personalities and relationships.
- Set up the second tutorial quest.

### Cinematic Beat

Tarin is attempting to impress several younger children by balancing a wooden sword on his finger. The sword falls and nearly hits him.

Lyra catches it with a small burst of wind magic.

### Dialogue

**Lyra:**

> “One day, Tarin, your greatest enemy will be your own sword.”

**Tarin:**

> “A true warrior must be ready for betrayal from any direction.”

**Kael:**

> “Especially from above, apparently.”

The three laugh.

### Character Interaction

Lyra asks whether Kael had another strange dream. Tarin suggests Kael's previous life must have involved being a legendary king.

**Kael:**

> “Why a king?”

**Tarin:**

> “Because no one ordinary complains as much as you do.”

### Quest Setup

Tarin's father needs help bringing practice weapons to the training field.

Lyra must gather herbs near the edge of the woods.

The friends decide to do both tasks together.

### Quest Assigned

**Quest: Friends and Favors**

### Objectives

- Carry practice weapons to the training field.
- Complete a basic sword lesson.
- Travel with Lyra to the herb meadow.
- Gather 4 silverleaf plants.
- Defeat or scare away 3 small slimes.

## Scene 7: Sword Training

### Scene Type

Combat tutorial

### Location

Village training field

### Characters

- Kael
- Tarin
- Daren or village guard instructor
- Several village children

### Story Purpose

- Teach basic combat.
- Establish that Kael is capable but inexperienced.
- Show Tarin's fighting style.
- Foreshadow Kael's unusual reflexes.

### Tutorial Mechanics

- Basic attack
- Heavy attack
- Blocking
- Dodging
- Lock-on targeting if used
- Stamina
- Training dummy interaction

### Sequence

1. The player attacks a training dummy.
2. The player blocks three attacks from Tarin.
3. The player performs a dodge.
4. The player completes a short sparring match.

### Scripted Moment

During the spar, Tarin performs a move Kael has never seen.

Time briefly slows.

Kael sees a faint blue path showing the sword's movement.

The player is prompted to dodge.

Kael avoids the strike perfectly.

Everyone is surprised.

### Dialogue

**Tarin:**

> “You cheated.”

**Kael:**

> “How?”

**Tarin:**

> “I don't know yet, but I'll think of something.”

Daren watches Kael carefully.

### Hidden System Introduction

This is the first subtle manifestation of Echo Sight during combat.

The game should not formally name the ability yet.

## Scene 8: The Herb Meadow

### Scene Type

Exploration and light combat tutorial

### Location

Meadow near the forest edge

### Story Purpose

- Introduce party movement.
- Teach gathering outside the village.
- Introduce simple enemies.
- Develop the friendship between the three characters.
- Add a mystery clue.

### Environment

- Tall grass
- Wildflowers
- A shallow stream
- Forest edge
- Old stone marker covered in moss
- Butterflies and small fantasy creatures

### Gameplay

The three friends gather silverleaf.

Small blue slimes emerge near the stream.

The slimes are not highly dangerous.

### Combat Roles

- Tarin fights in melee.
- Lyra provides a small healing spell.
- Kael uses the player's equipped weapon.

### Dialogue During Exploration

**Tarin:**

> “When I become a knight, I expect both of you to speak respectfully.”

**Lyra:**

> “Of course, Sir Falls-Into-Hay.”

**Kael:**

> “A legendary title.”

### Mystery Event

After the slimes are defeated, Kael notices the old stone marker glowing faintly.

When he touches it, Echo Sight activates.

### Vision

Kael sees:

- Armored figures wearing black sun symbols
- A girl surrounded by golden light
- A stone doorway beneath the earth
- A dragon's eye opening

The vision ends quickly.

Kael falls backward.

### Dialogue

**Lyra:**

> “Kael! What happened?”

**Kael:**

> “I saw something.”

**Tarin:**

> “Something dangerous?”

Kael looks at Lyra.

**Kael:**

> “I don't know.”

### Development Note

The black sun emblem in the vision should match the emblem later seen on the bandit leader's gauntlet.

## Scene 9: Return to the Festival

### Scene Type

Peaceful social sequence

### Location

Village square

### Time

Late morning to afternoon

### Story Purpose

- Give the player a reward period after the tutorials.
- Strengthen emotional attachment to Lyra and Tarin.
- Allow optional mini-games.
- Create contrast before the attack.

### Festival Activities

The player may participate in several optional activities:

#### Archery Game

Shoot targets for coins and a cosmetic item.

#### Apple Barrel Game

Time-based collection mini-game.

#### Practice Duel

Fight Tarin in a friendly rematch.

#### Cooking Stall

Help Mira prepare stew.

#### Fortune Teller

A mysterious traveler reads Kael's fortune.

### Fortune Teller Dialogue

> “You carry the shadow of one life and the heartbeat of another.”

> “Three paths are tied together.”

> “One will be taken.”

> “One will follow.”

> “And one will awaken.”

Kael asks what it means.

The fortune teller is gone when he looks away.

### Friendship Scene

The three friends sit near the well with festival food.

Lyra says she wants to visit the capital and study healing magic.

Tarin says he wants to join the knights.

They ask Kael what he wants.

The player receives a dialogue choice.

### Player Dialogue Choices

1. “I want to see the whole world.”
2. “I want to understand my dreams.”
3. “I want to protect this village.”
4. “I haven't decided yet.”

The choice can slightly influence future dialogue but should not lock the player into a permanent class.

### Lyra's Response

Regardless of the selection, Lyra says:

> “Then we should all leave together someday.”

This line becomes emotionally important after her kidnapping.

## Scene 11: The Bandit Attack

### Scene Type

Major cinematic and combat event

### Location

Briar Glen

### Story Purpose

- Deliver the opening story's inciting incident.
- Introduce real danger.
- Test the player's tutorial skills.
- Reveal Kael's first major supernatural ability.

### Attack Structure

Bandits enter from three directions.

Some ride horses through the main road. Others emerge from behind barns and storage sheds, showing that they infiltrated the village beforehand.

Smoke bombs create confusion.

The bandits are not trying to destroy the village. Their true objective is to capture Lyra.

### Player Objectives

1. Defend villagers near the square.
2. Defeat 3 bandit scouts.
3. Rescue a child trapped near a burning cart.
4. Reach Lyra and Tarin.

### Combat Difficulty

The fight should feel dangerous but manageable.

Bandit scouts use:

- Basic sword attacks
- Slow crossbow shots
- Smoke bombs
- Short stun attacks

### Environmental Hazards

- Burning wagon
- Panicked animals
- Falling festival decorations
- Smoke reducing visibility
- Bandits blocking roads

### Mid-Battle Cinematic

Kael reaches the square and sees Lyra using healing magic on an injured villager.

A bandit wearing a black sun emblem points toward her.

**Bandit:**

> “That's the one!”

Several bandits move toward Lyra.

Tarin attacks them but is knocked aside.

Kael charges forward.

Rook, the bandit leader, intercepts him.

### Rook Dialogue

> “Stay down, boy. This does not concern you.”

### Kael Dialogue

> “She's my friend.”

Rook and Kael briefly fight.

Rook easily disarms Kael.

### Echo Sight Awakening

Rook raises his sword.

Time stops.

The world turns dark except for glowing blue lines.

Kael sees several possible attack paths.

A voice from the previous world whispers:

> “Move.”

The player completes a short reaction sequence.

Kael avoids the strike, grabs a fallen weapon, and cuts Rook's gauntlet.

The black sun symbol is revealed.

Kael experiences a flash of the earlier vision.

### Rook's Reaction

Rook sees a faint symbol appear in Kael's eyes.

He looks genuinely shocked.

> “A Worldwalker?”

Before Kael can respond, another bandit throws a smoke bomb.

## Scene 12: Lyra Is Taken

### Scene Type

Cinematic

### Location

Village square and northern road

### Story Purpose

- Complete the inciting incident.
- Create an emotional failure that motivates the journey.
- Show that Lyra is important to the enemy.

### Sequence

The smoke clears.

Lyra is being pulled onto a horse.

She struggles and uses a burst of golden magic, knocking one bandit away.

A second bandit uses a rune-marked restraint that suppresses her magic.

Tarin tries to reach her but is injured.

Kael runs after the horse.

### Dialogue

**Lyra:**

> “Kael!”

**Kael:**

> “Lyra!”

Kael nearly grabs her hand.

Rook kicks Kael away from the horse.

### Rook Dialogue

> “If you follow us, you will die.”

### Lyra's Final Line Before Escape

> “Don't let them reach the ruins!”

The bandits flee toward the northern forest.

### Important Mystery

Lyra knows about the ruins because she saw a map or overheard the bandits while captured. This can be explained later.

### Cinematic Ending

Kael lies in the road as rain begins to fall lightly, visually echoing the opening cinematic.

The silver pendant around his neck glows.

---

## Scene 13: The Aftermath

### Scene Type

Emotional exploration and dialogue

### Time

Late afternoon

### Story Purpose

- Show the cost of the attack.
- Allow the player to process the event.
- Establish the rescue mission.
- Provide equipment and story information.

### Environment Changes

The peaceful festival area is damaged.

- A cart burns.
- Villagers treat the injured.
- Decorations are torn.
- Several buildings are damaged.
- The music is replaced by quiet strings and wind.

### Player Objectives

1. Check on Daren.
2. Check on Tarin.
3. Speak with Elder Bram.
4. Inspect the dropped black sun emblem.

### Daren Scene

Daren has a wounded shoulder but is alive.

He tells Kael not to pursue the bandits immediately.

**Daren:**

> “Anger makes every road look shorter than it is.”

**Kael:**

> “They took her.”

**Daren:**

> “I know.”

### Tarin Scene

Tarin blames himself.

**Tarin:**

> “I was right there.”

> “I couldn't stop them.”

The player can respond:

1. “Neither could I.”
2. “Blaming yourself won't bring her back.”
3. “Then help me fix it.”

Regardless of the choice, Tarin decides to go.

### Elder Bram Scene

Elder Bram examines the black sun emblem.

He says it belongs to a mercenary network called the **Ashen Hand**.

The Ashen Hand operates from hidden camps and abandoned ruins.

Elder Bram believes the bandits may be moving toward the old **Greyfen Ruins** north of the village.

When Kael mentions the word “Worldwalker,” Bram becomes uneasy.

### Dialogue

**Elder Bram:**

> “Some words are dangerous even when spoken quietly.”

> “We will discuss that when Lyra is safe.”

---

## Scene 14: Preparing to Leave

### Scene Type

Equipment and decision scene

### Location

Rowan cottage and village armory

### Story Purpose

- Prepare the player for the larger world.
- Provide starting gear.
- Create a meaningful goodbye with Kael's parents.
- Confirm Tarin as a party member.

### Daren's Decision

Daren initially refuses to let Kael leave.

Kael argues that the village guard will move too slowly and the bandits already have a head start.

Mira intervenes.

**Mira:**

> “You taught him to stand when others run.”

**Daren:**

> “I taught him to survive.”

**Mira:**

# 12. Roblox-Specific Design Recommendations

## Multiplayer Structure

The story can be designed for one to four players.

### Option A: Shared Protagonists

Every player is treated as a Worldwalker traveling with the same story party.

### Option B: Host Protagonist

The host player is Kael, while other players are additional village friends or summoned companions.

### Recommended Approach

Use a shared-player model where all players are Worldwalkers connected to the same event. Story dialogue can still focus on Kael as the narrative lead, but every player receives quest credit and meaningful combat participation.

## Cinematics

- Keep most cinematics under 90 seconds.
- Allow skipping after first viewing.
- Use camera pans, character animations, subtitles, and music cues.
- Return control to the player frequently.

## Checkpoints

Recommended checkpoints:

1. After waking in Kael's bedroom
2. After completing Harvest Morning
3. After sword training
4. Before the bandit attack
5. After Lyra is kidnapped
6. Before leaving Briar Glen

## Respawn Logic

During the peaceful prologue, players respawn at the Rowan cottage or village shrine.

During the bandit attack, players respawn near the most recent defended area.

## Quest Sharing

All party members should receive progress when:

- A quest item is collected
- A required enemy is defeated
- A cinematic trigger is activated
- A major NPC conversation is completed

# 16. Dialogue Style Guidelines

Dialogue should be:

- Emotionally direct
- Appropriate for teenagers
- Lightly humorous during peaceful scenes
- More restrained during serious scenes

# 18. Future Story Direction

The rescue arc can reveal that the Ashen Hand was hired to bring Lyra to an ancient ruin.

The ruin contains a sealed gate that responds to Lyra's magic and Kael's Echo Sight.

The apparent bandit story expands into a larger conflict involving:

- A secret magical order
- Ancient dragon seals
- Competing kingdoms
- Worldwalker legends
- A forgotten civilization

# 22. Final Creative Direction

The opening should feel like the first episode of an anime and the tutorial chapter of a role-playing game at the same time.

The peaceful village must be enjoyable enough that the player cares when it is attacked.

Kael, Tarin, and Lyra must feel like real childhood friends before Lyra is taken.

The player should finish the prologue with three strong motivations:

1. Rescue Lyra.
2. Discover the truth about Kael's previous life.
3. Explore the world beyond Briar Glen.

The final emotional promise to the player is:

> You were reborn into this world for a reason, but before you can discover that reason, you must save the friend who has always believed in you.
